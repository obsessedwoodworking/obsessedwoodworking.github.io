If you’re an avid cornhole player, you likely have your own set of cornhole boards. While these boards come in various designs and materials, they all require a protective finish to ensure their longevity and performance. Why Use a Cornhole Board Finish?#

A cornhole board finish, also known as a clear coat or polycoat, serves as a protective layer for the board. It safeguards the wood from rain, snow, UV rays, mold, and other elements that can cause damage over time. Additionally, a good finish gives the board a smooth surface, allowing the cornhole bags to slide effortlessly during gameplay. Cornhole boards are often considered works of art, with custom designs and personalized touches. Therefore, it is crucial to maintain and protect their aesthetic appeal.

The Benefits of Polyurethane#

Polyurethane is the best finish for cornhole boards, as it offers numerous benefits. It creates a polished and smooth surface that enhances gameplay by allowing the bags to slide easily. Polyurethane also provides a water-resistant finish, protecting the boards from moisture-related damage. There are two types of polyurethane finishes to choose from: oil-based and water-based. Both options offer durability and easy application, but the choice ultimately depends on personal preference and specific project requirements.

Tips for Applying and Maintaining Your Chosen Finish#

  • Clean the cornhole boards thoroughly before applying the finish to ensure proper adhesion.
  • Follow the manufacturer’s instructions for application, including drying times and number of coats.
  • Use a high-quality brush or applicator to achieve a smooth and even finish.
  • Allow the finish to cure fully before subjecting the boards to any heavy use or outdoor conditions.
  • Regularly clean the boards with soap and water to remove dirt and debris.
  • Inspect the boards periodically for any signs of wear or damage and reapply the finish as needed.

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Cornhole Board Finish#

  1. Durability: Look for a finish that can withstand heavy use, harsh weather conditions, and UV rays.
  2. Ease of Application: Choose a product that is easy to apply and provides a smooth finish without streaks.
  3. Drying Time: Consider the drying time of the finish to ensure it fits within your project timeline.
  4. Compatibility: Check if the finish is compatible with the type of wood used for your cornhole boards.
  5. Odor: Some finishes may have a strong odor during application and curing, so consider your preferences and the environment in which you will be working.
  6. Price: Determine your budget and choose a finish that offers good value for money.

Investing in a high-quality finish for your cornhole boards is essential to protect them from the elements and maintain their appearance. By considering the key factors mentioned above and our top picks for the best finishes in 2023, you can ensure that your cornhole boards remain in top condition for years to come. So, choose the finish that suits your needs and enjoy countless hours of fun with your friends and family playing cornhole!
