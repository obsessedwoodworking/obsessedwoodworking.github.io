Table Saw vs Track Saw vs Circular Saw: Which Should You Buy?

June 22, 2023 · 4 min · 700 words · Gladys Henry

When it comes to choosing between a circular saw, track saw, or table saw, it can be a challenging decision to make. Each tool has its own advantages and limitations, and understanding them is crucial in determining which one is the right fit for your needs. In this article, we will discuss the key differences between these three types of saws, and by the end, you will have a better understanding of which one is best suited for you.

Let’s start by looking at the circular saw. One of the most basic options for ripping with a circular saw is using a straight edge as a guide. This method allows for almost unlimited cutting length, as long as you have a straight edge long enough. However, the downside is that it can be time-consuming to set up the cut each time you make a new one. To simplify the process, tools like the Kreg Rip Cut can be used to set a distance and repeat the cut as many times as needed.

Moving on to the track saw, it offers a faster setup compared to a circular saw. Instead of clamping down a straight edge, you simply strike a line and lay the track on it. The main limitation of a track saw is the length of the track, but this can be overcome by purchasing a longer track or attaching multiple smaller tracks together. Track saws provide speed and repeatability, making them suitable for ripping multiple pieces to the same width.

Now, let’s introduce the Craig Adaptive Cutting System (ACS), which is a unique tool that combines the benefits of a table saw and a track saw. Unlike traditional table saws where the blade stays stationary while the workpiece moves through it, the ACS keeps the workpiece stationary while the blade moves through it. This system offers versatility as the track can be detached from the table, allowing for the same functionality as a track saw. The ACS provides speed and repeatability, making it a solid option for ripping multiple pieces to the same width.

Finally, we have the table saw, which excels in terms of speed and accuracy. Table saws allow for quick and precise cuts, making them ideal for projects that require precision. However, as the size of the workpiece increases, it can become more difficult to control, and in some cases, it may be more practical to bring the tool to the workpiece instead of vice versa. Table saws are great for making tiny adjustments as you work towards a perfect fit.

When it comes to cross-cutting, the table saw offers speed and repeatability, making it suitable for various projects. The ACS comes close to the table saw in terms of speed and accuracy, offering perpendicular holes that can be used to make perfect 90-degree corners. Track saws can also be used for cross-cutting, but they require more setup and lack the repeatability aspect.

For more intricate cuts, a circular saw can be used, although it may require additional setup and tools such as a speed square. The ACS, track saw, and table saw can also handle angle cuts, with each option offering its own advantages. The ACS allows for adjustable angles and is suitable for smaller pieces, while a track saw can be made more accurate with attachments like an adjustable track square. Table saws are ideal for repeatable angle cuts and can handle larger pieces more effectively.

While a circular saw can perform dado grooves or rabbets, it is not the most practical tool for these tasks. Both track saws and the ACS can handle these cuts, but the ACS provides a more realistic option with its versatility and ease of use.

In conclusion, the choice between a table saw, track saw, or circular saw depends on your specific needs and the projects you plan to tackle. Consider factors such as speed, accuracy, repeatability, and the size of the workpieces you will be working with. Each tool has its strengths and limitations, so understanding them will help you make an informed decision. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned woodworker, finding the right saw for your needs is essential for successful and efficient projects.
