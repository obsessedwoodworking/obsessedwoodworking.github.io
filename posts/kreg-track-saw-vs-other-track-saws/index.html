If you’re in the market for a track saw, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we will be comparing three popular track saws – the WEN, the Kreg, and the Festool. We will examine their features, performance, and value for money, so you can make an informed decision when purchasing a track saw that fits your needs and budget.

Breakdown of Features#

Each track saw comes with its own set of features and warranty, so let’s break it down:

WEN Track Saw#

  • Cutting capacity: 0 to 45 degrees
  • Maximum cutting depth: 1 5/8 inches at 45 degrees, 2.3 inches at 90 degrees
  • Motor power: 10 amp brushed motor
  • Warranty: 2 years

Kreg Track Saw#

  • Cutting capacity: 0 to 45 degrees
  • Maximum cutting depth: 1 1/2 inches at 45 degrees, 2 1/8 inches at 90 degrees
  • Motor power: 12 amp brushless motor
  • Warranty: 2 years

Festool Track Saw#

  • Cutting capacity: 0 to 47 degrees
  • Maximum cutting depth: 1 11/16 inches at 45 degrees, 2 8/8 inches at 90 degrees
  • Motor power: Brushless motor
  • Warranty: 3 years, all-inclusive

Pricing and Additional Accessories#

When it comes to pricing and what you get with each track saw, here’s the breakdown:

WEN Track Saw#

  • Price (with track): $179
  • Additional accessories: Track and cardboard boxes

Kreg Track Saw#

  • Price (with track): $419
  • Additional accessories: Carry bag

Festool Track Saw#

  • Price (with track): $800
  • Additional accessories: Batteries, charger, and sustainer box

Build Quality and Ergonomics#

The build quality of each track saw is an important consideration. Here’s what you need to know:

  • WEN Track Saw: Despite its lower price point, the WEN track saw offers decent build quality. However, there is some blade deflection when beveling or cutting at angles other than zero.
  • Kreg Track Saw: The Kreg track saw has a robust build quality and offers excellent grip with its slightly larger handle.
  • Festool Track Saw: Known for their high-quality tools, Festool provides a track saw with top-notch build quality and ergonomics.

Key Features and Accessories#

Each track saw comes with unique features and accessories that enhance functionality:

  • All three saws have an anti-kickback feature to ensure safety during use.
  • The Kreg and the Festool have tool storage in the handle for easy access during blade changes.
  • The Festool track saw has an electronic block brake that stops the blade within two seconds.
  • The Kreg track saw features a riving knife that prevents board pinching during cuts.
  • Depth stops are available on all three saws, with the Festool offering the easiest adjustment mechanism.
  • The Kreg track saw has markings for three-quarter inch plywood, making accurate cuts easier.
  • The bevel gauges on the Kreg and the Festool are superior, offering better visibility than the WEN.

Comparison of Tracks#

When comparing the tracks of each track saw, here’s what you should know:

  • The WEN track is 50 inches long, and two tracks are included in the package, allowing for a total length of 100 inches.
  • The Festool track is 55 inches long, providing a good balance between portability and cutting capacity.
  • The Kreg track is the longest at 62 inches, allowing for full-depth plunge cuts and ripping through multiple sheets of plywood.

Performance on Different Materials#

To give you a better idea of how these track saws perform, let’s look at their cutting performance on plywood and hardwood:

  • In a test with three-quarter inch plywood, all three saws performed well. However, the WEN track saw required backing up off the track to cut the very end of the plywood.
  • When it comes to cutting hardwood, all three track saws handled the task with ease, offering clean and accurate cuts.

Conclusion and Recommendations#

After examining the features, pricing, and performance of the WEN, Kreg, and Festool track saws, it’s clear that each one has its strengths and weaknesses. Here are our recommendations:

  • If you’re on a budget and looking for a reliable track saw, the WEN is a great option.
  • If you value build quality and excellent grip, the Kreg track saw is a solid choice.
  • If you’re willing to invest in a top-of-the-line track saw with superior performance and a longer warranty, the Festool track saw is the way to go.
