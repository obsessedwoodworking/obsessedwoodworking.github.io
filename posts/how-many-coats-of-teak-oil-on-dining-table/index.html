How to Use Teak Oil for Wood Finishing | Achieve Stunning Results with Furniture Clinic

August 12, 2023 · 3 min · 556 words · Jackie Carlson

When it comes to finishing wood, using an oil is a great option for providing protection against daily use, weather, and moisture. In addition, oil can enhance the natural beauty of the wood, making it a popular choice among woodworkers. In this article, we will focus on the use of teak oil from Furniture Clinic and explain how to achieve stunning results with this product.

Teak oil, formulated with a traditional recipe of pure tung oil and linseed oil, is a safe and non-toxic option for finishing wood. It is particularly recommended for garden furniture, windows, doors, cladding, and fencing, as it provides UV protection and helps slow down the aging process of not only teak but also other types of exterior and interior wood. This oil is ideal for replacing lost oils due to weathering, bringing back the rich golden honey color of the wood while preventing drying, warping, and splitting. Despite its name, teak oil is suitable for all types of wood and dries to a lovely matte finish, preserving the natural look of the grain.

The overall final finish of teak oil depends on the type of wood it is applied to. Different types of wood, such as beach, oak, and walnut, will react differently to the oil, resulting in varying degrees of darkening. To ensure satisfaction with the results, it is advisable to test the oil in an inconspicuous area before applying it to the entire piece. This way, you can determine if the desired effect is achieved.

Applying teak oil is a simple and straightforward process. Start by ensuring that the wood is clean and dry. Then, apply the oil to a lint-free cloth and gently wipe it into the wood, following the direction of the grain. The viscosity of the teak oil makes it easy to apply and allows it to penetrate deep into the wood fibers. Any excess oil can be wiped away after a few minutes of application. If additional coats are desired, wait for the previous coat to dry before applying another layer. This will help build up layers of protection for the wood. The drying time for teak oil is relatively quick, with a recall time of only four to six hours. To achieve a professional finish, lightly sand the surface between coats with fine sandpaper to denib and smooth it.

It is recommended to annually reapply teak oil to maintain the wooden items. This will ensure they are well-maintained and continue to look their best. By following these simple steps, you can easily use teak oil to achieve stunning results on your wood projects.

In conclusion, teak oil from Furniture Clinic is a fantastic option for wood finishing and offers a range of benefits. Its formulation with pure tung oil and linseed oil ensures a safe and non-toxic solution for protecting wood against daily wear and tear, weather, and moisture. The oil brings out the natural beauty of the wood, enhances the grain, and provides a matte finish that highlights the richness of the wood’s color. By following the application process outlined above and regularly reapplying teak oil, you can maintain your wooden items and enjoy their beauty for years to come.
